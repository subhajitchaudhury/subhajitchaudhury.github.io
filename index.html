About me

I am a Research Scientist at IBM Research, Tokyo working on Reinforcement Learning and Computer Vision. I obtained my Ph.D. from the University of Tokyo working with Prof. Toshihiko Yamasaki on the topic of robust deep learning methods against adversarial attacks. I received my master's degree in Electrical Engineering from IIT Bombay in 2014 working with Prof. Subhasis Chaudhuri. I recieved my bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Jadavpur University in 2012. .

Work Experience

  • IBM Research, Tokyo, Japan
    Position: Research Scientist [Apr 2017-Present]
    At IBM Research, I work on reinforcement learning algorithms applied to dialog-based systems and imitation learning from videos. Additionally, I am also involved in applied research for weakly supervised event detection from videos.

  • NEC Research Laboratories, Japan
    Position: Researcher [Oct 2014-Mar 2017]
    At NEC, I worked on deep learning based crack detection from videos. Our crack detection system was used for inspections of road cracks in Texas, USA. Additionally, our system was used to find defects on the runaway in the Nanki-Shirahama airport in Wakayama, Japan.

Subhajit Chaudhury, Hiya Roy

Implemented land-cover classification from hyperspectral satellite images using convolutional neural networks. Results demonstrate that CNN based classification performs considerably well compared to traditional feature classifiers like support vector machines.

The Music Box : Computer Graphics animation in OpenGL from scratch.
Subhajit Chaudhury, Urmi Sadhu

Modeled an articulated human figure using basic geometry transformations, prepared a virtual environment, provided appropriate lighting and texture maps to finally create a full animation in OpenGL from scratch.
Estimating disparity map from stereo images

Shape from Shading Using Surface Normals
